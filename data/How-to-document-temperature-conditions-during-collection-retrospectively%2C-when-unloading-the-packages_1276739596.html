CADIS Knowledge Base : How to document temperature conditions during collection retrospectively, when unloading the packages

Available as of release R 2024.2

cadis TCL does not yet offer temperature monitoring during the collection process in the way you know it from loading: As a driver, you cannot yet scan a package "into a vehicles compartment" during collection. 
Consequently, without the feature described here, the temperature curve in the temperature report would not visualize the temperature of packages during their the first mile transport from the shipper to the depot.
However, when unloading collected packages that require temperature monitoring, cadis offers a feature for monitoring the first mile transport of unqualified collections retrospectively, during unloading.

Use case and constraints

As a driver, you drive a last mile trip with one or more unqualified collection stops. At the collection stop, you have no access to the compartment of the vehicle, because

  • you collect a trailer that has already been loaded by the shipper

  • or you have to wait until the shipper has finished loading the vehicle.

In cadisAPP, you only document the collection stop as ‘collected’, without scanning the packages. When you’ve finished your trip, you return to the depot.

At the depot, someone unloads the vehicle by scanning the packages.

Required configurations

Create unloading with pickup (sorry for the wording!)
in order to get collections included in the unloading lists of the corresponding trip when the collection stop is finished.

  • in cadisSTUDIO, open “Administration”

  • select “Configuration Manager” and then “Transport Execution”

  • select “Trip handling”

  • Select “Create unloading with pickup”

Loading unit thermo assignment required
for cadisAPP to have the user document the previously unknown vehicle compartment (i.e. scan the barcode on the vehicle compartment) for collected packages contained in the unloading list.

  • in cadisSTUDIO, open “Administration”

  • select “Configuration Manager” and then Unloading

  • click “LOCAL_TRAFFIC

  • below “Temperature”, select “Loading unit thermo assignement required”

Document temperature monitoring in cadisAPP

Precondition: Collection documented on stop level

When collection EDIs are not complete and you don’t have access to the shipper’s quay, you document the collection on stop level by marking the collection as “Collected OK” without scanning any package.

Unloading: Postactively document the collected packages’ vehicle compartment

Temperature Report

  1. Open cadisCONTROL

  2. Open “Shipment overview”

  3. Open the context menu of the unloaded shipment

  4. Select “Print” and then “Temperature report”
