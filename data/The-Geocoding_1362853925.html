CADIS Knowledge Base : The Geocoding

Created by Julian Beyer, last modified on Sep 08, 2025

The geocoordinates of delivery stops can be automatically defined in cadis or set manually by the dispatcher.

Why geocode?

Geocoding stops is an important action to:

  1. Benefit from accurate data: your drivers will identify the exact location of your delivery stops, thus, minimizing delivery mistakes. Plus, dispatchers can also find the exact location of stops on the map, in cadis back-office

  2. Optimize your trips: the sequence optimization is based on the Geo position of shipments. If shipments part of your trips are not geocoded, the sequence optimization cannot happen.

  3. Use the Geofence based features: for more info, see our article here

  4. Use the cadis stops management features: for more info, see our article here

  5. Track your trips advancement in cadis back-office: for more info, see our article here

  6. For drivers, have cadisAPP check their position at delivery: for more info, see our article here

How to Geocode a stop

In cadis a Stop can be geocoded in 3 ways: the Automatic geocoding, the manual geocoding, the StopID match.

The automatic geocoding

Your delivery stops will be automatically geocoded in cadis, if the stop address as sent by your host system is accurate enough. This means the data should include a:

  • Country code

  • City

  • Postal code

  • Street name

  • Street number

If the shipment’s data is not complete enough or cadis could not automatically geocode, the task can be performed manually.

The manual geocoding

You can manually geocode a shipment if it was not automatically geocoded or no match was done with the StopIDs.

In the list of shipments

In the list of shipments, double click on a shipment to open its details:

❶ The addresses of involved parties are shown
❷ You can also open the tab addresse where in addition, you’ll be able to the geo-coordinates
❸ If the stop was already geocoded in the past, you will find an additional row “Delivery address (geocoded)”
❹ To geocode the stop, click on the target icon top left of the screen, then choose delivery or collection address

❶ CADIS may suggest one or many geocoded addresses for your stop. You can select one of them & click on apply
❷ In the address bar, you can also adjust the address data to generate additional suggestions
❸ You may also click directly on the map to create a new geocoded address, then apply
