CADIS Knowledge Base : The Recurring Collections

Created by Mohamed.Shebl, last modified by Julian Beyer on Sep 08, 2025

cadis allows you to create recurring collections that will be generate daily, answering specific attributes & if necessary, on a defined period of time.

How to create your recurring collections

Access to the collection templates

Create your collection template

❶ The field for Shipper is the only mandatory field. If you enter directly the shipper’s name or use the magnifying glass icon, cadis will search for the shipper’s data in your Stop Locations master data (see The Stops Management section 2.1 for more info). If the shipper’s data have never been entered, you can click on the pen icon to enter his data manually.

❷ You can give a name to your template (free text) & assign it already a departure template

❸ Many other attributes can be setto your collection template (temperature sesnitive, dangerous or long goods…) Scroll down to find them.

❹ See details below for the rest of tabs

Complete your collection template

In the tab Schedule You can define

  • a period of time for the validity of your collection

  • the time slots your drivers can present themselves at shipper’s location

  • Deactivate temporarily your collection

The tab Goods Items help you define the kind of equipment needed to proceed with the pick-ups (europallets, containers…). Enter also the weight & dimensions for better estimation of needed space in your vehicles

You can set Instructions. It is possible to have them applied during processes other than collection (at unloading…)

Generate your collections

Collections will be generated from your template automatically every day, around midnight. Nonetheless, you can generate them manually

In cadisCONTROL, open the Last Mile Loading menu, click on “Recurring collections/trips”, choose the date & click OK. The collection will be generated; you can find it in the list of shipments.

In the collection details above, the Instructions & time slots defined are well reported. The planned trip is the one we defined in the templates.
