Difference between “Maintained” and “Technically usable”

In cadisAPP, “maintained” Android versions are those we actively maintain, ensuring compatibility, security, and regular updates. These versions are fully tested, and we provide dedicated support for troubleshooting and assistance.

Conversely, “technically usable” Android versions that are not “maintained” still function within our system, but they are not officially supported. While you should be able to still use these versions, we cannot guarantee their continued functionality, and support is limited.

Please be aware that changes in Android guidelines or software requirements could mean that “technically usable” versions become incompatible at short notice.

Android Version Policy

While Android OS doesn’t follow a fixed release cycle, a new version is typically launched each fall. Often, these updates require adjustments in cadis to ensure all functionalities work as expected, or in some cases, to maintain basic operability. The effort needed to make cadis compatible can vary greatly, from minimal tweaks to significant technological changes.

This primarily impacts users of consumer devices, as manufacturers of industrial (ruggedized) devices typically follow a delayed release policy for OS updates. In general, consumer devices adopt new Android versions faster, with high-end models being the first to ship with the latest OS or to offer upgrades on existing devices.

To enable cadis as a BYOD (Bring Your Own Device) solution on as many consumer devices as possible, our aim is to support each new Android OS version by spring of the following year at the latest.

In addition to new versions, Android regularly discontinues support for older versions and restricts apps using outdated libraries from the Google Play Store due to evolving security and privacy standards. Consequently, we may also need to phase out support for legacy Android versions in cadis.

As there are thousands of Android devices on the market with even more potential current and historic Android OS version (and vendor specific derivative versions) it is impossible for any developer of Android applications to test proper functionality anything but a small minority of them. This is especially true for ruggedized industrial devices and accessories that require vendor speicific device drivers. The most important example of this regarding cadisAPP is barcode scanners. A current list of supported and maintained industrial devices from our hardware partners is available on request.

If you’re considering new hardware for use with cadis, we strongly recommend testing it first with cadisAPP to ensure it meets your performance and usability requirements (such as for scanning). While cadisAPP is highly likely to work with your chosen device, a quick test can provide added assurance. The app installs in under a minute from the Google Play Store, allowing you to register the device directly to your cadis SaaS instance or, if accessible from the internet and up to date, your on-premise installation. If needed, we’re happy to assist by providing access to our cadisAPP demo environment for testing.

Please note that cadis only supports standard Android OS. Specialized or modified versions, such as “Android One,” are not supported.
