CADIS Knowledge Base : The Rules Engines

Created by Mohamed.Shebl, last modified by Julian Beyer on Sep 08, 2025

cadis offers 9 rules engines, build for you to automate your daily operations, especially dispatching shipments, setting priority levels, selecting planned positions or implement handling instructions

 Access from cadisSTUDIO

❶ Auto-attribute a level of priority to your shipments

❷ Automate assignment of planned Terminal Locations to your shipments

❹ Automatically assign handling instructions to shipments

❸ Automate dispatching of shipments & Collections to trips

Principles of rule creation

❶ Create categories of rules by selecting the blank field & naming your category

❷ Click on « Add rules » to add new rules to your category

❸ A relation of hierarchy between rules can be set by drag & drop.

Note that the higher the rule is placed in your scheme, the higher the priority t’ll get

❶ Click on « Add rule »

❷ Choose an argument, a criteria & an expected result. You can click on “Add value” to add additional expected results. Then select a departure & if the Auto dispatch is set to “TRUE” shipments will be automatically dispatched to the chosen departure as soon as cadis receives their EDI form your host system.

❸ Click on “Save”

❹ The rules of an agency can easily be imported from / exported towards another agency under a format recognized by cadisSTUDIO

❺ Click on the context menu to toggle from “If” to “If Not”

The shipments execution date

The execution date is the date when a shipment I supposed to be processed. CADIS will not auto-dispatch it until the execution date comes.

After editing your rules engine, right on selected shipments to change their execution date. Only then, those will be dispatched according to your new rules

Simulate with a shipment

❶ Click on « Simulate »

❷ Enter your shipment number & search

❸ Pass your mouse cursor on the green icon to read it’s meaning
