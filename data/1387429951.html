CADIS Knowledge Base : Temperature Control (TCL)

Created by Mohamed.Shebl, last modified by Julian Beyer on Sep 08, 2025

Temperature Control (TCL) is a Licensed features in cadis which allows to retrieve & display the temperature data monitored by your sensors in your vehicles & quays. This service stretches from 1st collection at shipper to last delivery at consignee.

Business Process

❶ The driver proceeds normally with collections, at shipper’s location

❷ It is at unloading in agency that cadisAPP users can document the retroactive monitoring of temperature at collections

❸ cadisAPP can allow or constraint users to report the shipments (un)loading location. This is to ensure a complete reporting of temperature monitoring

❹ You can print your shipments temperature report at any time. It proves to all stakeholders the proper temperature maintenance

Configuration & set-up

In the ConfigManager

The Telematics

cadis interfaces with your sensors providers to retrieve the data measured in your vehicles & terminal compartments. The configuration is ensured by cadis team of system integrators

The checks

(Un)Loading checks can allow you to minimize human mistakes during those sensitive steps. Indeed, cadis compares your shipments data & compartments temperature range. If a shipment is (Un)loaded towards the wrong compartment, the user gets an alert, potentially forbidding him to proceed further in the app

In the Temperature Control Menu

The Sensors tab

❶ Print the barcodes of one or more sensor in PDF format. These barcodes are used in the process of loading & collection unloading

❷ You can wiew the packages & shipments details monitored by the sensor you have selected

❸ Click on a sensor’s row to display an overview of the temperature data. The associated icon allows you to enlarge the image

❹ You can read in this overview the number of packages with temperature range “2°-8°”, monitored by the selected sensor

❺ The temperature curve can be saved as an image

The Terminal compartments tab

In this tab, you will be able to read all Terminal compartments in all agencies. This is intentional, it allows quality management to have a real overview in sensors use

❶ Click on “Create” for a new Terminal compartment or double click on an existing one to edit it

❷ Name your compartment (free text)

❸ Select from a drop-down list the sensor monitoring your compartment. The content is filled with data retrieved by cadis from your providers

❹ Select the range of temperature of your compartment & click on Save

In the EDIs

The temperature monitoring & related checks in cadis rely on data sent from your host system through the Electronic Data Integration (EDIs).

❶ Set to 1 to see the icon for thermo-sensitive goods appear in cadis screens. It is only for informative purpose, no checks will be made.

❷ Define the Temperature range of your shipments. They can be for example 2°-8° for cold goods & 15°-25° for warm. Or any other range for individual temperature

Document compartments in cadisAPP

At Loading

❶ In the above loading list, 2 shipments with a different temperature range, should be loaded

❷ cadis proceeds to 2 checks with related alert messages :

  1. The scan of a package without documenting the vehicle compartment

  2. The scan of a package for which the shipment’s temperature range differs from the compartment temperature ranges

❸ To document a vehicle compartment, scan a barcode respecting format: cTF_TCLS_PROVIDER_DATALOGGER_SENSOR (see the section “sensor’s tab” where you can print a pdf document of the vehicle compartment)

❹ Select the temperature range of the vehicle compartment. It can be changed even after 1st validation, simply by clicking on the banner (see next screen)

❺ The banner shows the selected temperature & will change color depending on it (blue = cold, orange = warm)

Also, the sensor’s reference is displayed at package level after it has been scanned for loading. At any time, you can scan a new compartment barcode & scan the packages loaded in it.

At Unloading

❶ In the above unloading list, 2 shipments with a different temperature range, should be unloaded

❷ & ❸ As for the loading, cadis checks if:

  1. A quay compartment is documented before package scan

  2. The temperature range of the package & the quay compartment should match

❹ & ❺ To document a quay compartment, click on the list of options at list level, then « Edit storing position ». Select your Storing Position. You can scan it under format:

cTF_STP_STORING POSITION

❻ The banner shows the Storing Position. It is also shown at package level to indicate their position on quay. At any time, you can repeat steps ④ & ⑤ to change the storing position of a package.

Print the temperature report

❶ The temperature required for this shipment is shown

❷ Important steps are marked on the chart (Collection, loading, delivery & unloading)

❸ Packages included in the shipment

❹ Shipment number barcode

❺ Difference between the desired temperature threshold and the temperature actually reached during the excursion

❻ The Temperature history is updated every 5 minutes

❼ Sensor reference (one curve is shown per sensor)
